What is agile?

“Agile” describes a way of working where self-organising and cross-functional teams can develop evolving solutions which adapt to changing environments, requirements and new learnings and ideas. The Agile methodology was formalised in 2001 with the Agile Manifesto. But a lot of the Agile concepts are not new! In fact, most of us have probably applied it in some way when doing high school science projects.

12 principles based on the manifesto were created as a guideline to help development teams follow the agile methodology. These include costumer satisfaction, changing requirments, frequent delivery, regular communication, supporting team members, face-to-face communication, measure work progress, development process, good design, measure progress, continue to seek results as well as reflecting and adapting frequently.

So, why might we use Agile? As humans, we're used to sticking with rigid structures and linear steps. But in most projects or processes, things are likely to change in ways we didn't consider or know about when we first started. With agile methodology, we emphasise adaptability and incremental improvements to our solution. So, if our business is disrupted by external or internal factors, we're better able to respond.

MOSCOW Prioritisation

Dynamic System Development Method (DSDM) is an agile project method which focuses on the overall project lifecycle. We can use it to help produce quick and efficient results for the organisation. One of DSDM practices is MoSCow.

MoSCoW is a prioritisation technique used to help comprehend and manage priorities in order to track progress and meet deadlines. The technique can be used for requirements, tasks and user stories.
The capatilised letters represent the level of priority:

Must have

Should have

Could have

Won't have
